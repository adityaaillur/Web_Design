The etymology of heavenly Kashmir is traced to mythological Vedic Sage "Rishi Kashyapa" who meditated there for a long time. Following Abhinav Gupta - Philosophy, Logashree - Astronomy, Charaka - Ayurveda, Sushrutha - Medical Science. Panini - Grammar, Vateshwar - Tiginimetry, Suyya - Hydraulic Engineerng, Kalidas - Sanskrit Sarangadeva - Musicology, Kalhana - History, Vishnu Sharma - Moral Science (Panchatantram), Bharat Muni - Natyashastra etc... Such great Pandits made Kashmir a Nirvana.

Picturesque and enchanting, Kashmir is cradled high in the lofty green Himalayas and hailed all over the world for its incredible natural beauty. Surrounded by mountain peaks, lush green valleys, glistening lakes, There are a total of 1,842 Hindu places of worship in Kashmir including temples, shrines, holy springs, holy caves and holy trees.

Of the 952 temples, 212 are running while 740 are in a dilapidated condition, according to government figures, it has inspired poets through centuries.
